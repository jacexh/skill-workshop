# Workshop stories

## Stories

1. As a program planner, I want to open or cancel a workshop so registrations
   are offered only while the program is viable.
2. As an attendee-services coordinator, I want to commit or release a seat and
   promote the next waitlisted attendee when capacity becomes available.
3. As a delivery coordinator, I want to record a participant's attendance or
   no-show and correct that evidence when the facilitator reports an error.
4. As a facilitator-pay coordinator, I want to grant or revoke an earning when
   accepted delivery evidence changes the facilitator's right to payment.

## Observed business language

Interviews use the word "confirmed" in every story: a workshop is confirmed
when it may open, an attendee is confirmed when a seat is committed,
attendance is confirmed when delivery evidence is accepted, and an earning is
confirmed when it becomes payable. Each fact can later change without
automatically reversing the other three, and the four operators decide only
within their stated responsibility.

The stories do not establish whether "confirmed" is one shared business fact
or several facts with different meanings and decision authorities. They also
do not establish which accepted facts should cross between those
responsibilities. No domain topology or cross-boundary contract has been
accepted.
