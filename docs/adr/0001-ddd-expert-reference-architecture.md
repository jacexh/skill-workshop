# ddd-expert uses progressive reference knowledge leaves

- Status: Accepted
- EventStorming modeling and artifact-write decisions superseded by: [ADR 0003: EventStorming Is an Adversarial HITP Modeling Process](0003-event-storming-whole-model-confirmation.md)

The ddd-expert plugin separates modeling authority, artifact execution, artifact structure, and reusable knowledge. EventStorming is the single user modeling workflow from backend evidence to either an explicitly requested ready Strategic Model or a codify-ready Tactical Design. Big Picture and Process Modelling feed one convergence loop, while Software Design EventStorming is its tactical zoom, so semantic or tactical pressure can revisit the earlier model without a skill or phase return. A material Scenario Thread converges through triggering facts and visible information, actor and intent, decision and established fact, affected rights or obligations, reactions, and a stable outcome. Modeling probes close causal gaps with facts or counterexamples; decision proposals are reserved for user-owned alternatives that change a business-visible guarantee, not approval of a notation or DDD label. EventStorming exclusively authorizes authoritative Strategic Model facts and resolved, justified Tactical Design writes, while Codify/Guard may inspect only; an evidence-supported DDD representation does not require a separate user-approval ceremony. The internal maintain-artifacts skill validates authority, minimum consistency closure, accepted Model and Design slices, readiness status, and model-design revisions without owning semantic decisions. Artifact templates distinguish accepted `evolving` content from `shape_ready` Models and `codify_ready` Designs. References own DDD concepts, house rules, verified technology guidance, and concise navigation. This keeps artifact mechanics and modeling iteration behind one interface without pretending that maintain-artifacts creates a trusted caller boundary or separate agent.

Guard has an explicit review topology. Its main coordinator freezes one Review Envelope, launches independent read-only workers for Design Realization and House-Style Conformance concurrently, merges their candidates by reason family, verifies the evidence, and owns final routing. A Codify handoff uses a fresh read-only coordinator distinct from the implementer; the implementer supplies the scoped request and either immutable base/target identifiers or an immutable base plus a complete fingerprinted worktree snapshot, while the coordinator independently freezes the review inventory. That inventory gives stable IDs to every scoped Design obligation, changed file, required production path, and additive layer, mechanism, or specialized-surface label. Worker coverage rows map back to those IDs, and both axis unions must reconcile with no missing or unknown inventory IDs before completion. Before clearing, the coordinator revalidates both the source snapshot and artifact fingerprints; drift leaves the execution incomplete. Workers attach evidence detail only to non-clear rows and cannot delegate. Each unresolved merged family receives at most one bounded depth worker, but the coordinator never substitutes either complete required axis. An axis that still cannot complete after one retry, or a depth worker that cannot produce a terminal result in its single dispatch, leaves the Guard execution incomplete. The shared skill describes these roles and ordering without binding them to one code agent's delegation API.

The canonical common leaves are ddd-modeling, ddd-core, and ddd-collaboration, with database as the single Persistence House Style. Go is the primary implementation path: its compact baseline routes to Layer Guides for Domain, Application, Transport, and Infrastructure; Flow Guides for CQRS, events/messages, and taskqueue; and Platform Guides for scaffold and Runtime. Lower-frequency Python and TypeScript support remains in one compact language guide each, organized into focused sections rather than separate leaves. Files remain flat under references, and the Claude and Codex plugin tracks stay mirrored.

House Rules are conditional on their stated concern but mandatory once applicable. Design-changing mechanisms require an accepted decision; covered implementation concerns use the prescribed stack. Concrete code and SQL examples stay with the leaf that owns the rule, while evaluation fixtures and workflow checks stay outside references.

The former ddd-agent-contract and ddd-modeling-gates files are retired. Useful modeling probes move into ddd-modeling; SKILL navigation points directly to the smallest relevant leaf. README inventory, link checks, mirror checks, focused contract assertions, and external eval cases protect this architecture without pinning whole paragraphs or line counts.
